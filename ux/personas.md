# CostablancaBridge — User Personas

**Version:** 1.0 · April 2026
**Owner:** UX Lead

These four personas represent the primary user segments CBB serves. Every UX decision should be tested against at least one of them. When designing a page or flow, ask: "Does this work for James? Would Sofia understand this? Would Mohammed find this trustworthy? Would Erik know what to do next?"

---

## Persona 01 — James

### The UK Business Owner Entering the EU

**Name:** James Whitfield
**Age:** 52
**Location:** Manchester, UK
**Role:** Managing Director, 35-person manufacturing consultancy

### Context
James built a successful consulting firm that helped European manufacturers optimise operations. Pre-Brexit, 40% of his clients were EU-based. Since 2020, he has lost EU contracts because his firm has no EU legal presence and cannot easily bid on EU-funded projects. He knows he needs a Spanish subsidiary but has been putting it off for two years because it seems complicated.

### Goals
- Establish a legally compliant Spanish SL that lets him contract with EU clients
- Ensure his two EU-based employees (Netherlands, Germany) are correctly employed
- Understand his tax obligations in Spain without hiring a permanent local accountant
- Do this without spending 6 months on paperwork

### Pain Points
- Previous attempt to engage a Spanish law firm: slow, expensive, communication breakdown
- Doesn't speak Spanish and is worried about getting lost in translation
- Doesn't know what he doesn't know — he needs a partner who anticipates the questions he hasn't asked yet
- Concerned about the ongoing cost of compliance after the setup

### Behaviours
- Researches thoroughly before contacting anyone — will read the whole website
- Searches Google for "set up company in Spain after Brexit" and "Spanish subsidiary UK company"
- More likely to book a call than fill out a contact form
- Trusts case studies and specific credentials over generic "here's what we do" copy
- Decision-maker — no committee approval needed, can move fast once trust is established

### What Wins James
A clear, step-by-step explanation of how CBB handles formation + ongoing compliance as a package. A specific case study from a UK firm similar to his. A fixed-fee or clearly explained pricing model. The ability to book a 30-minute discovery call without filling out a long form.

### Journey Stage at Arrival
Usually Discovery or Consideration — he has researched CBB before arriving. Will consume 3–4 pages before contacting.

### Key Screen: Homepage
He needs to see "UK companies post-Brexit" called out explicitly. If he doesn't see his situation reflected in the first 10 seconds, he will leave.

## Persona 02 — Sofia

### The Digital Nomad Founder

**Name:** Sofia Andersson
**Age:** 34
**Location:** Bali (currently), originally Stockholm
**Role:** Founder, UX consultancy, fully remote

### Context
Sofia runs a profitable 4-person UX studio entirely remotely. She has been living outside Sweden for three years and realised she needs a proper EU base — both for client credibility and for banking (she keeps getting rejected by payment providers who want an EU business address). She has heard Costa Blanca is becoming a digital nomad hub and wants to explore it.

### Goals
- Get Spanish residency (Digital Nomad Visa or Entrepreneur Visa)
- Register a Spanish SL with a real bank account and EU IBAN
- Potentially relocate herself and her partner to Alicante within 6 months
- Find local co-working and business community connections

### Pain Points
- Has been to three government websites and gotten completely confused about which visa she qualifies for
- Tried to open a Spanish bank account remotely — impossible without an NIE
- Doesn't want to relocate and then discover she made a mistake with her tax residency
- Needs everything to be doable from abroad (she can't fly to Spain to attend a meeting next week)

### Behaviours
- Highly mobile, heavily online — discovers CBB via Instagram/LinkedIn or nomad community forums
- Reads blog content and personal case studies (wants to see "someone like me" who went through it)
- Comfortable with digital forms and online video calls — wants everything remote-first
- Price-sensitive but not penny-pinching — will pay for quality and clarity
- Shares good services with her network — high word-of-mouth potential

### What Wins Sofia
A clear visa comparison tool or guide ("Which visa is right for me?"). A relocation timeline showing 30–60 days from start to residency. A case study from a remote founder. Remote-first onboarding — no requirement to be physically present in Spain until necessary.

### Journey Stage at Arrival
Usually Discovery — she finds CBB via content or recommendation, curious but not yet committed.

### Key Screen: Relocation / PEOPLE Pipeline page
She needs a clear breakdown of the visa options and what the 30–60 day process looks like. If she can see a timeline, she'll book immediately.

## Persona 03 — Mohammed

### The Emerging Market Trade Investor

**Name:** Mohammed Al-Rashid
**Age:** 47
**Location:** Casablanca, Morocco (with regular trips to Dubai and London)
**Role:** Director, agricultural export and mineral trading company

### Context
Mohammed's family business exports phosphates and agricultural goods. He has been trying to break into the EU market for four years but keeps hitting regulatory walls — his goods need EU customs handling, EMA compliance in some cases, and a legitimate EU distribution entity. He has met several consultants who didn't understand the deal structure well enough. He needs someone who speaks both his commercial language and EU compliance.

### Goals
- Establish a Spain-based import SPV that can handle EU distribution of his goods
- Find a credible partner who will co-invest in the structure alongside him (not just charge fees)
- Get regulatory compliance in place that will survive EU customs scrutiny
- Protect his family's commercial interests with proper legal architecture

### Pain Points
- Tired of generic "we help companies enter the EU" consultancies who don't understand trade structures
- Previous advisors always escalated to lawyers who then charged £500/hour and produced nothing actionable
- Needs confidentiality — his trade routes and supplier relationships are proprietary
- Wants a principal partner who is financially aligned with making the deal work, not just billing hours

### Behaviours
- Arrives via referral or LinkedIn — does not respond to cold outreach
- Will research CBB deeply before making first contact — checks LinkedIn, looks for credentials
- First contact will likely be a personal email or LinkedIn message, not a website form
- Meetings will be formal and substantive — he will test CBB's knowledge in the first conversation
- Values direct, confident communication — hates waffle and excessive caveating

### What Wins Mohammed
The Principal Model section — this is what differentiates CBB from every other firm he's spoken to. The CIS & Emerging Market credentials (wife's background in Russian steel/pharma, founder network in North Africa). A clear deal structure walkthrough — how an SPV is formed, how CBB participates, how compliance is maintained. Confidentiality handled explicitly.

### Journey Stage at Arrival
Usually Consideration or Ready — he arrives knowing what CBB does. He wants proof and a direct path to a substantive conversation.

### Key Screen: How We Work / Principal Model page
He needs to understand the equity/carried interest structure in detail. If the site only shows service tiers without the co-investment angle, he will not contact CBB.

## Persona 04 — Erik

### The Nordic High-Net-Worth Relocator

**Name:** Erik Lindqvist
**Age:** 63
**Location:** Stockholm, Sweden
**Role:** Retired partner, engineering consultancy. Net worth: €2–4M

### Context
Erik took early retirement two years ago. He and his wife have been visiting Costa Blanca for ten years — they love the climate, the food, and the quality of life. They've finally decided to make the move permanent. He wants a Non-Lucrative Visa, a home purchase in Jávea, and to ensure his pension income is structured tax-efficiently for Spanish residency.

### Goals
- Obtain Non-Lucrative Visa for himself and his wife
- Purchase a €600K–€800K villa in Jávea or Denia
- Understand the Spanish tax implications of his Swedish pension income
- Be fully settled (legally resident, bank account, healthcare) within 90 days
- Find a reliable local contact for ongoing administrative support

### Pain Points
- Had a bad experience with an estate agent who bundled in "free legal services" that turned out to be conflicts of interest
- Worried about making a tax mistake that will haunt him for years
- His Spanish is limited to conversational level — needs professionals who speak English
- Wants a single point of contact who handles everything, not being passed between specialists

### Behaviours
- Researches through Google and expat forums (Expatica, various Facebook groups)
- Will compare 3–4 service providers before deciding
- Values clear pricing over cheap pricing — wants to know what he's paying for
- More comfortable with phone calls than forms — may call before booking online
- Highly risk-averse — will read every testimonial and look for guarantees

### What Wins Erik
Clear pricing or price ranges for the visa and setup package. A testimonial or case study from a retired couple in a similar situation. The 30–60 day relocation guarantee with a clear timeline. A human contact he can speak to. Tax guidance framed around his specific situation (pension income, Swedish residency change).

### Journey Stage at Arrival
Usually Consideration — he has found CBB through research and is comparing services. Needs social proof and pricing clarity before contacting.

### Key Screen: PEOPLE / Relocation Launchpad page + About page
He wants to understand who is behind CBB and what their credentials are. The legal background of the co-founder will be specifically reassuring to him.
